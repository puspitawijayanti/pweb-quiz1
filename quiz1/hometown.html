Kediri

The Spirit of Java

Overview

Kediri is a district located in the East Java Province, Indonesia. Kediri Regency is divided into 26 districts. Furthermore, its geography is flanked by two mountains of different nature: Mount Kelud to the east, which is volcanic, and Mount Wilis to the west, which is non-volcanic. Meanwhile, right in the center of Kediri Regency runs the Brantas River, dividing the regency into two parts: the western part of the Brantas River, which consists of the hilly slopes of Mount Wilis and Mount Klotok, and the eastern part of the Brantas River.

Highlights

  • History: Initially, the name Panjalu was better known than Kadiri. The Panjalu Kingdom then gradually expanded and became known as the Kediri Kingdom, a large kingdom whose history remains renowned to this day.
  • Economy: Kediri's main industries are sugar and cigarettes, making the city a production center for these two commodities in Indonesia.
  • Culture:
    • Jaranan: A traditional dance deeply rooted in Kediri, showcasing the dancers' skillful imitation of horse movements.
    • Wayang Krucil: A puppet show that combines local folklore and has become a popular attraction.
    • Wayang Kulit and Wayang Orang: Wayang performances tell epic stories such as the Mahabharata or Ramayana, with a strong pre-Islamic cultural acculturation.
  • Access: To get from Surabaya to Kediri, there's a bus service several times daily, usually operating from Bungurasih Terminal. Another option is to take the train from Surabaya Station (SGU) to Kediri.
